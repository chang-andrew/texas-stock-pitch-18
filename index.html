About Texas Stock Pitch

TSP is a chance for students who have practiced the art of investment research and presentation to show their skills to a panel of judges comprised of seasoned industry professionals from around the nation, representing investment banks, private equity firms, hedge funds, and more.


Students will have the opportunity to compete for thousands of dollars in prize money via the Best Freshman Team and Top 3 Overall Team prizes.

Choose

Form a team and choose a publicly traded company.

Research

Research your company of choice. Let us know why your company will succeed (or fail).

Pitch

Pitch your company thesis to a panel of esteemed judges.







Competition Details

Each team of 4 members will pitch an equity, long or short. Pitches are evaluated based on quality of idea, analysis, and presentation. First round pitches are to be 12 minutes followed by 5 minutes of Q&A from the judging panel. Teams that advance to the final round will again have 12 minutes to pitch and 5 minutes of Q&A.


In order to be pitched at Texas Stock Pitch, your security must meet the following requirements as of market close on March 8, 2019:

  • Market capitalization above $500 million
  • Share price above $2.00
  • 3-month average daily trading volume above 100,000 shares
  • Must be traded on the NASDAQ, NYSE, or NYSE AMEX
  • Security must be a common or preferred equity and not an ETF, ETN, Closed-End Fund, derivative, currency, debt instrument, or commodity
  • Pair trades are acceptable as long as each individual security meets the previous requirements


Register your team here before February 26th, 2019 at 5PM for a spot in the competition. Teams will be capped at 30 and priority will be given to teams from outside the University of Texas at Austin.

About USIT

The University Securities Investment Team is the premier investing organization at The University of Texas at Austin. Each year, it hosts three state-wide competitions: Texas Charity Pitch, Texas Stock Pitch, and Texas Shark Tank. Learn more by visiting our website at texasusit.org.
